As we wrap up an incredible first semester with Frugal Innovations in Sustainable Healthcare (FISH), we wanted to take a moment to reflect on everything we’ve accomplished, and to share our excitement for what’s ahead.

## A Huge Thank You to the Parents Program

We are beyond grateful to the Parents Program for helping fund our club’s activities, events, and early device-development efforts. Their support has opened doors for us that would have been impossible otherwise, from giving our design teams the resources they need to explore real clinical problems, to helping us grow as a community of innovators. We truly could not do this without them, and we are so thankful for the trust they’ve placed in our mission.

## A Strong Semester of Innovation

This fall, our members dove deep into healthcare challenges, explored the principles of frugal engineering, and began shaping ideas that have the potential to improve accessibility, sustainability, and patient care. Our General Member Meeting (GMM) presentations were a huge success! Each team showed impressive research, thoughtful problem breakdowns, and genuine passion.

We were proud to see:
- Clear communication and confident presenting
- Creative visualizations and engaging slides
- Strong connections to frugality, sustainability, and accessibility
- Personal stories, physician insights, and real-world relevance woven into each pitch
- Deep research into existing technologies, gaps, and opportunities
- Early design thinking and feasibility considerations
- And most importantly: teamwork, curiosity, and a willingness to learn

The feedback was constructive, encouraging, and full of excitement for where these ideas might go. Every team demonstrated tremendous growth and dedication, and we couldn’t be prouder of the work presented.

## Looking Ahead

Next semester, FISH officially enters the design and development phase. Our teams will begin prototyping, testing approaches, and working more closely with clinicians—including our new connections with physical therapists at the Fried Center, who have already begun sharing real problems we can help solve.

We have big plans on the horizon: more events, more hands-on opportunities, more collaboration across disciplines, and more updates to share soon. This is just the beginning.

## Stay Tuned

Thank you again to the Parents Program, our members, and everyone who has supported FISH so far. This semester has shown us exactly what can happen when passionate students come together to build solutions that matter.

Be on the lookout for design team updates next semester, we cannot wait to show you what’s coming.
